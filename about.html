Our Story

From Humble Beginnings

Bristol Foods Kikoni began in 2015 as a small street food stall near Makerere University, serving hungry students our signature Rolex at affordable prices. What started as a way to pay tuition quickly grew into a beloved local spot, thanks to our commitment to quality ingredients and bold flavors.

In 2018, we opened our first proper restaurant opposite Nalika Hostel, expanding our menu while staying true to our roots. Today, we're proud to be one of Kampala's most popular spots for authentic Ugandan street food with a modern twist.

Our name "Kikoni" (meaning "kitchen" in Luganda) reflects our philosophy - every dish is prepared with the same care and attention as if we were cooking for our own family in our home kitchen.

Our Mission

At Bristol Foods Kikoni, we're on a mission to:

  • Celebrate Uganda's vibrant street food culture while innovating with global flavors
  • Source 80% of our ingredients from local farmers and producers
  • Provide affordable, high-quality meals that everyone can enjoy
  • Train and employ youth from our community
  • Reduce food waste through careful planning and composting

We believe good food should be accessible to all, which is why we've kept our prices student-friendly while never compromising on quality.

Community Involvement

We're proud to be more than just a restaurant - we're an active part of our community:

  • Weekly donations to Nalika Hostel's student food program
  • Sponsorship of local football teams
  • Free culinary training for underprivileged youth
  • Annual "Taste of Kampala" festival participation
  • Partnership with urban farming initiatives

Every month, we host "Community Nights" where 10% of proceeds go to a local charity.
